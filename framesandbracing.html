Module 8: Frames and Bracing

We've looked at gears for about 3 modules, but what happens when they slip? They shouldn't. In this module, you'll learn about frames and how they prevent slippage. We'll also look at a technique called bracing, which can help make a structure stronger.

Frames

Frames secure gears together to avoid making them slip. They are absolutely necessary to avoid mistakes. Without frames, moving parts on your robot wouldn't work well. Bracing

Bracing is used to effectively resist a structure on your robot against torsion. Torsion is when a part is stressed and rotated unevenly. It is the twisting of an object due to applied forces from outside. Think of rotating an axle from the ends in opposite directions. That's torsion! Due to how LEGO uses strictly discrete units (studs) for measurements, you usually can't build your entire robot with bracing. However, bracing can be used well for borders on a robot, and it greatly prevents liftarms from twisting. We'll talk about borders around a robot in Module 9.

The reason why you won't find a lot of people talking about this is because it's subtle. But, it is a crucial technique when applying this to areas that need lots of support. It's for structure.

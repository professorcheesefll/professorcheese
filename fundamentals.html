Here are the Fundamentals

These informational modules are designed for beginner and intermediate FLL teams, but they can be used by anyone! They aren't lectures, but can develop your knowledge from the basics to something more advanced.

By the end, you will be able to:

  • CAD on Studio 2.0
  • Understand gears and gear ratios
  • Understand gear frames and reslient structures
  • Create a robot ready for FLL

Module 1: Engineering Design Process

Description:

  • Making problem-solving easier by thinking like an engineer
  • Seperating a design process into digestible stages
    • Identify the problem (so it doesn't bite you later)
    • Research the problem (so you are informed)
    • Brainstorm solutions
    • Plan a solution (to visualize what you're going to build)
    • Create/Build a prototype
    • Test the prototype
    • Improve what didn't work
    • Share the results
  • Estimated time: 5 minutes

Module 2: Using Studio 2.0 for Planning

Description:

  • Building on Studio 2.0 before doing it in real life
  • Learning how to use the amazing features of Studio 2.0
    • Dragging items from the "shapes" menu
    • Searching for an item using the "search bar"
    • Using the tools (Select, Hinge, Move, Hide, Clone, Connect)
    • Getting comfortable with connecting parts
  • Estimated time: 20-30 minutes

Module 3: SPIKE Prime and LEGO Geometry

Description:

  • Owning your kit
  • Identifying sensors and important sections in LEGO Spike Prime
    • The gyro sensor
    • The color sensor
    • The distance sensor (ultrasonic)
    • The force sensor
  • Look deeper into what each type of pin does
    • The friction pin
    • The non-friction pin
  • Estimated time: 10 minutes

Module 4: Gears

Description:

  • Identifying the "driver gear" and "follower gear"
  • Connecting gears together to transfer movement
  • Exploring how LEGO's geometry allows for gears to be connected in specific ways
    • Some gears can only connect with certain other ones
  • SPIKE Prime's available gears (found in the kit)
      Obtaining a SPIKE expansion kit for more freedom
  • Estimated time: 15 minutes

Module 5: Gear Ratios

Description:

  • Exploring the idea of gear ratios and what they represent
  • Understanding the types of gear ratios that exist
    • Torque
    • Speed
  • Gaining intuition on how torque and speed are created
  • Understanding the strengths of torque and speed
  • Applying the right gear ratio to a tool or frame
  • Using a gear ratio calculator
  • Estimated time: 30-40 minutes

Module 6: More Gear Types

Description:

  • Acknowledging that there are more gear types that can be used to create unique gear trains
    • Spur gears
    • Bevel/Crown gears
    • Double bevel/crown gears
    • Worm gears
    • Knob gears
  • Understanding non-gear pieces that are also splendid
    • Gear racks
  • Estimated time: 40 minutes

Module 7: Pulleys

Description:

  • When gears are bad
  • Why pulleys exist
  • When pulleys are effective
  • Why pulleys can be bad
    • Belt slipping
    • Somewhat limited nature of official LEGO elastic belts
    • Elastic problems
    • Breakage (rare)
  • Estimated time: 15 minutes

Module 8: Frames and Bracing

Description:

  • Learning how to secure multiple gears without them slipping
  • Using common pieces for frames and bracing to hold gears together well
  • Testing which form works best for a specific task
  • Estimated time: 10 minutes

Module 9: Building a Robot

Description:

  • Finding out what makes a robot good
    • Low center of gravity
    • Weight-distribution towards the back
    • Lots of surface area where tools could be placed
    • Ability to go around the entire FLL field without deviating from the set path
  • Testing your robot
  • Maintaining your robot for competitions
  • Estimated time: 1 hour

Module 10: Building Tools and Attachments

Description:

  • Finding out what makes a tool or attachment good
  • Connecting your robot and tools together as modules
  • Using the fewest number of tools possible to complete the most missions
  • Reliability checking
    • Does the tool work 20/20 times?
    • Are your technicians able to fit it on the robot quickly?
    • How long does it take to complete the mission?
  • Estimated time: 50 minutes

Module 4: Gears

Gears transfer rotational movement from one location to another by interlocking their teeth. In this module, we'll specifically explore driver and follower gears, why gears have teeth, SPIKE Prime's own default gears and how you can expand them with the SPIKE Expansion kit.

Using the Proper Terminology

Referring to the features on a gear with the right words is important because:

  • If every teammate uses the same words for a thing, there's way less confusion.
  • Once you start using the right words, you'll also know what each feature does.
  • Judges will understand what you are trying to say during a robot design presentation because you speak in the same language they do.

Driver Gear vs. Follower Gear

Look at the gear train below.

The driver gear is the gear that initiates the motion. That means it is the gear that is spun by the motor (in FLL's case).

The follower gear is the gear that gets turned by the driver gear. It "follows" the driver gear.

In this case, that means the black gear in the image is the driver gear because it is being rotated by the motor, while the tan gear is the follower gear. Having a driver gear and a follower gear isn't restricted to perpendicular connections.

Gear Teeth

You've probably noticed that gears have these sharp, jagged, and pointy spikes around them. These are called teeth. They make a gear train more effective by providing:

  • Grip and friction with surrounding connected gears
  • Provide consistent spacing between any gears
  • Provide smooth and consistent rotational motion

Knowing the amount of teeth a gear has is important later on when we reach module 5.

SPIKE Expansion Set

The SPIKE Expansion Set has more parts and gears to choose from, and can significantly increase the amount of contraptions you can build. Since it is standard practice for many teams to already have this set, here are some steps to add it to Studio 2.0:

Step 1

Click on "Import LEGO Sets" in Studio's home.

Step 2

Search for "45681" and you'll see the SPIKE Expansion Set.

Step 3

Add the set to Studio by clicking on "Import as a palette".

Step 4

You should now have more gears to choose from in your palette when you've selected "45681-1 SPIKE Prime Expansion Set".
